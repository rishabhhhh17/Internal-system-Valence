01The problem

The firm's knowledge is everywhere, and therefore nowhere.

A mid-sized deal at a boutique advisory touches 20+ documents, 10+ counterparties and three or four team members across two time zones. None of it lives in one place. A junior needs to know who Valence pitched in EdTech last year — the answer exists, but it's in an MD's email thread from nine months ago.

Where the time goes

  • Hunting through email and Drive for a memo the firm wrote six months ago
  • Rebuilding precedent comp tables from scratch because no one logged them
  • Walking a new analyst through a live mandate for four days before they're useful
  • Losing pitch context when the one senior who ran the last meeting leaves
The firm already has the knowledge. It doesn't have the retrieval.
02The insight

Boutique advisory doesn't need more software. It needs one surface.

Off-the-shelf tools are built for scale — Salesforce for 5,000 reps, DealCloud for a 200-person bank. They bury a three-person firm in configuration. At the other end, spreadsheets and Gmail don't scale beyond the person who set them up.

The gap in the middle is institutional. A firm that runs fifteen mandates a year, across two cities, with a small senior team, needs something in between — opinionated, fast, and shaped around the five motions that actually matter: pipeline, knowledge, the day, analytics, people.

Don't build a CRM. Build the firm's memory, surfaced every morning.

The product belief

  • Every question an analyst asks should get a cited answer in under 30 seconds
  • Every mandate should be one drawer, not thirteen tabs across three apps
  • Every morning should open with the day already read to you
  • Every metric a partner wants should be one page away, not a Saturday spreadsheet
03The product

ValenceOS — six pages, one operating layer.

Every page is designed around a moment in the day of someone running a deal.

1
Overview — the morning briefing
A chief-of-staff card: greeting, a three-sentence AI-generated day summary, today's meetings, open tasks, free slots for tap-to-propose meetings. Walk in, know the day.
2
Deal Logger — pipeline as one drawer
Kanban by stage. Every card opens into a drawer with 13 tabs: brief, files, checklist, deal team, counterparties, activity, financials, CIM, similar deals, data-room shares, target list. One surface per mandate.
3
Knowledge — ask the firm, cited
Two tracks. Shared: memos, files, comps, deal notes, searchable semantically, askable in plain English. Every answer cites the source. Private: your personal Drive, same search, never leaves your account.
4
Day Planner — calendar that thinks
Google Calendar integration. Today's meetings with counterparty context. Free-slot grid with one-tap email-the-invite to any counterparty — Gmail + Calendar invite go out together.
5
Analytics — IC-grade, in one page
Five sections: pipeline (funnel + conversion + aging), composition (sector × stage, size, fees), productivity (velocity, win rate, bankers), forward (4-quarter forecast, book curve, what-if), coverage (clients, geography, hygiene). Period + sector filters scope everything.
6
Team — coverage at a glance
The directory — senior coverage across Mumbai and London, filterable by sector and role. A one-click reference when you need the right person for a mandate.
04How it works

A single day, through the system.

09:00
Open the Overview
MorningBriefing pulls your Google Calendar + Supabase tasks + meetings. Gemini writes a three-sentence summary. The pulse strip shows active pipeline and expected fees. You know the day before the first coffee.
10:00
Diligence call on Nimbus Health
Open the deal drawer. AI brief auto-generated from counterparty history, notes, and files. Similar past deals surfaced from pgvector embeddings. Walk into the call with context.
11:30
Analyst asks: "what have we done in EdTech?"
Knowledge → Ask. "EdTech mandates and comps, last 24 months." Cited answer in 12 seconds — three memos, two closed deals, and a comp table. The junior is productive on day one.
14:00
Propose a meeting to a buyer
Day Planner → FreeSlots. Tap 15:00 Thursday. A Gmail draft opens with the counterparty, a Calendar invite attached. Confirm once. Done.
18:00
Partner asks: "how are we tracking to the fee goal?"
Analytics → target gauge. 62% of $5M annual target already committed. 4-quarter forecast charts the rest. What-if slider models a 10% uplift on Diligence conversion. Answer in 30 seconds, not Saturday.
05The AI layer

Where the firm's data becomes the advantage.

Every page works without AI. The AI is what makes it feel different from any off-the-shelf tool a firm could buy — because no off-the-shelf tool has the firm's memos.

What ValenceOS knows

  • Every memo, file, comp, deal note ever logged — indexed with pgvector embeddings
  • Every counterparty interaction — who pitched whom, when, at what valuation
  • Every mandate the firm has run — stages, durations, fees, close reasons
  • Every banker's sector surface area — inferred from deal ownership and memo authorship

How it shows up

  • Ask — plain-English questions, answers grounded in firm-shared sources, every claim cited
  • AI briefs — auto-generated pre-meeting summaries in the deal drawer
  • Similarity — when a new mandate lands, the system surfaces the three most similar past deals
  • Morning summary — a three-sentence chief-of-staff read of the day ahead
  • What-if — analytics scenarios modeled in real time, not in a spreadsheet
No other tool has these memos, this calendar, these deal notes, indexed together. The firm's own data is the moat.
06Why this, why now

Boutique advisory is underserved between Excel and DealCloud.

What exists today

  • DealCloud — built for 200-banker desks, priced out of reach
  • Salesforce — generic CRM, fifteen weeks of config to fit an IB workflow
  • Affinity — relationship intelligence, no deal drawer, no data-room
  • Shared Drive + Gmail — the default, does not scale past five people
  • WhatsApp — where the firm actually lives, impossible to audit

ValenceOS

  • Opinionated for the 3–20 person advisory shop
  • Ships with the firm's five motions pre-wired, zero config
  • Every page integrates with Gmail + Calendar + Drive out of the box
  • AI grounded in firm memos from day one — not a feature, the product
  • One interface, one drawer, one ask — no tab sprawl

The shape of the opportunity

  • Post-pandemic, mid-market advisory is the fastest-growing slice of IB fees globally
  • Boutiques are hiring faster than enterprise tools can be configured for them
  • AI retrieval crossed a usability threshold in 2024 — grounded citation is now table stakes
  • Every boutique partner already trusts one internal system — their inbox. ValenceOS replaces it.
